Our Services

Soil Sampling

Soil sample testing analyzes the chemical, physical, and biological properties of soil to determine nutrient levels, pH, and other factors affecting plant growth and overall soil health.

Agronomy

Agronomy is the science of soil management and crop production, encompassing the study of plant biology, soil science, weed management, and the use of technology like GPS and drones to improve agricultural practices. It focuses on producing food, feed, fuel, and fiber for human needs, using plants in agricultural systems.

Nutrient Tracking

In agricultural contexts, understanding and managing the flow of carbon and nutrients is crucial for both improving soil health and addressing climate change. Nutrient tracking tools and practices are used to optimize nutrient use, minimize environmental impacts, and potentially sequester carbon in soils.

Drone Application

Drones are increasingly used in agriculture for a variety of tasks, including crop monitoring, precision spraying, seeding, and livestock management. These applications improve efficiency, optimize resource use, and provide farmers with valuable data for informed decision-making.

Pivot Injected

Pivot irrigation, also known as center-pivot irrigation or circle irrigation, is a method of crop irrigation where a long pipeline rotates around a central pivot point, delivering water to crops via sprinklers. This creates a circular irrigated area, often resembling crop circles when viewed from above.
